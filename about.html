About me

Cooper Le-Doux

Biomedical engineering researcher at the intersection of neuroscience, machine learning, and brain-computer interfaces.

Building at the edge of brain & machine

I'm a biomedical engineering graduate student at the University of Colorado Boulder (M.S., expected May 2027), with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from New Mexico State University — where I also minored in Biomedical Engineering, Computational Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and Chemistry.

My core focus is non-invasive EEG signal analysis and developing machine learning models that extract meaningful patterns from brain data. Through my work at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, I built an independent brain-computer interface project from scratch, improved signal processing pipelines, and explored deep learning approaches including CNNs and decision tree models for VR simulation.

I'm fluent in Python — working regularly with MNE-Python for EEG, PyTorch and scikit-learn for modeling, and matplotlib and NumPy for data analysis. I also have hands-on research experience spanning cryptographic analysis, MATLAB automation (cutting microbead analysis time by 90%), cell culture, microscopy, and flow cytometry.

Beyond research, I've taught undergraduate biology labs at CU Boulder, instructed English internationally, and volunteered as a blacksmith demonstrator — experiences that shaped my ability to communicate complex ideas accessibly. This site reflects both what I've built so far and where I'm headed. I believe in learning by doing, and I welcome collaboration, feedback, and mentorship.
