The Team

This project was a fully collaborative effort. All five team members contributed equally to the design, simulation, economic analysis, and documentation of this capstone.

About the project

Equal contribution, shared authorship

This capstone was completed as part of the Chemical & Materials Engineering program at New Mexico State University, advised by Dr. Brewer and Dr. Andersen. The team designed a complete industrial-scale plant from feedstock to final product, covering process design, Aspen simulation, economic analysis, EHS considerations, and facility layout.

Every section of the report reflects collective iteration and review. Individual focus areas are listed below, but all decisions were made as a team.

Process Design & Simulation

Process Design & EHS

Focused on the theoretical process flow diagram development, environmental health and safety (EHS) considerations for oleum and ammonia handling, and the overall process description documentation.

TL
Taylor Lee
Separation Units & Energy Balances

Developed the separation strategy (distillation column T-1 and centrifugal phase separator S-1), designed the heat exchanger network using pinch analysis, and led energy balance calculations across the process.

ES
Estrella Sedillo
Economic Analysis & Costing

Led equipment cost estimation using CAPCOST, fixed capital investment analysis, operating cost breakdown, and the NPV comparison between palladium and nickel catalyst scenarios. Developed the cash flow diagram.

BS
Benedek Szalai
Simulation & Material Balances

Contributed to Aspen model validation, mass balance table development, molecular bond structure corrections for uncommon species, and documentation of simulation assumptions and errors.
