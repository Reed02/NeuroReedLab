Motivation

Why biomass-derived caprolactam?

ε-Caprolactam is the monomer for nylon-6, used in textiles, carpeting, and industrial applications worldwide. Today's production is almost entirely petroleum-based. Phenol can instead be extracted from lignin — the second most abundant biopolymer on Earth — offering a genuinely renewable feedstock that reduces fossil fuel dependence and associated emissions.

Renewable feedstock

Phenol sourced from lignocellulosic biomass via lignin conversion — replacing crude-oil-derived benzene hydroperoxide routes.

Closed-loop design

Unreacted cyclohexanone, hydroxylamine sulfate, and water are all recycled back into the process — minimizing raw material costs and waste streams.

Economically viable

Palladium catalyst yields a positive NPV of $18.4M over 15 years. At $3.00/kg sale price, the plant breaks even within 10 years including 2 years of construction.

Process at a glance

Four-reactor pathway

R-1

Phenol hydrogenation → Cyclohexanone

Biomass-derived phenol and hydrogen gas are fed into a plug flow reactor (PFR) packed with palladium catalyst. High conversion and continuous flow make the PFR ideal for this catalytic gas-phase step.

450 °C 1–2 bar Pd catalyst PFR
R-2

Oximation → Cyclohexanone oxime

Cyclohexanone reacts with hydroxylamine sulfate in a CSTR. Uniform mixing suppresses side reactions (e.g. cyclohexanol formation). The effluent is split via distillation (T-1) and centrifugal phase separation (S-1) — unreacted cyclohexanone and hydroxylamine sulfate are both recycled.

85 °C 1–2 bar CSTR
R-3

Beckmann rearrangement → ε-Caprolactam

Purified cyclohexanone oxime undergoes Beckmann rearrangement with fuming sulfuric acid (oleum) in a CSTR. Complete mixing ensures high yield and supports continuous industrial throughput. Modeled in Aspen as 3 CSTRs in series to capture accurate kinetics.

90 °C 1–2 bar CSTR × 3 Fuming H₂SO₄
R-4

Ammonia neutralization → 98.5% ε-Caprolactam

Crude caprolactam is treated with ammonia in a final CSTR, neutralizing residual sulfuric acid and impurities. This yields ≥ 98.5% pure product and ammonium sulfate as a co-product — purified and sold to fertilizer companies, creating an additional revenue stream.

100 °C 1–2 bar CSTR Ammonium sulfate co-product

Key Design Decisions

What shaped this design

Palladium over nickel

Despite Pd costing ~$15,000/kg vs ~$85/kg for Ni, palladium's far higher selectivity to cyclohexanone dramatically reduces the mass needed and downstream processing — yielding NPV of +$18.4M vs −$54.1M for nickel.

PFR for hydrogenation

A plug flow reactor allows catalytic packing, high conversion in a single pass, and is well-suited to continuous vapor-phase operation — ideal for the phenol + H₂ reaction at 450 °C.

Heat exchanger network

Pinch analysis and a 9-exchanger HEN reduced total utility usage from 89,091 MJ/h to 36,134 MJ/h — a 60% reduction — cutting annual utility costs from $3.33M to $2.44M.

Albuquerque, NM site

Located at 87120 with proximity to I-40 for raw material delivery and product distribution. Site layout accounts for New Mexico's high-wind environment and grid reliability challenges.
