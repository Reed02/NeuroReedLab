Muse 2


Muse 2 Website

Features:

- Slim design, suitable for comfortable commercial usage.
- Adjustable for different head sizes
- USB Charging
- 4 EEG channels + 2 auxiliary channels. 
- PPG (heart-rate) sensor
- Accelerometer
- Electrode Positions: TP9, AF7, AF8, TP10; reference electrode FPz (256 Hz sample rate, resolution 12-bit)
- Accelerometer: three axis @ ~52Hz, 16-bit resolutions, +4G range.
- Materials: Flexible polycarbonate body, conductive gold electrodes.

Intended Usage/Thoughts:

The goal originally was set in learning how to interpret and clean EEG signals using a low cost EEG device. The Muse 2 has the additional benefit of being comfortable and not overly bulky. However, it soon became apparent that my goal may not be in improving the predictive models based on EEG signals but rather, can multimodal behavioral and physiological signals predict decision and movement behaviors. This widens my range from only EEG technologies to a wide variety of technologies including but not limited to Functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS), Eye Tracking, Galvanic Vestibular Stimulation (GVS), Electrocardiography (ECG) and Electromyography (EMG). 


I anticipate that the MUSE 2 will work well for Prefrontal Cortex EEG readings. This may be helpful for detecting blinks, level of focus/fatigue, and some physical movements. The Muse Company's appeared intent for the product seems to be on improving meditative states, and detecting movement, and relaxation levels. This supports my thoughts that the product will likely prove effective for detecting focus, and some eye movements. 

Paired with something like Eye Tracking, the Muse 2 could prove highly effective at creating predictive models of user intent based on where the user is looking and how focused or distracted they are.
